# Pet Printables — Free Pet Portrait Giveaway

## Social media content kit + creative direction

**Mechanic in one sentence:** Upload a pet photo → get a free shareable portrait → automatically entered to win the canvas version.

**Brand-voice non-negotiables** (applies to every piece in this kit):
- Never use *hand-painted*, *hand-finished*, *artist-made*, *hand-drawn*, or any craft claim. Product is **AI-generated**, instant.
- Never use *art*, *masterpiece*, or *fine art*. Use **portrait**, **print**, **keepsake**, **memento**.
- Never show prices on top-of-funnel creative. ARV ($109.99 CAD) lives only on the rules page + email gate (legal requirement).
- Never crop the pet's head or ears. `contain` over `cover` in any thumbnail/frame.
- *Made in Canada*, never *Made in Toronto*.

**One single tracked URL to use everywhere:** `https://petprintables.ca/pages/free-pet-portrait?utm_source=<platform>&utm_medium=<format>&utm_campaign=canvas-giveaway-may26`

## AI video prompts (paste into Sora / Veo 3 / Kling / Runway Gen-3)

These are deliberately detailed so the generators don't drift. Adjust pet breed to match what you want to feature. Keep all prompts pet-safe — no risky positions, no AI-trying-to-render-eyes-too-close (notorious failure mode).

### Prompt 1 — "Pet glow-up" transition (9:16, 6s)
> A young golden retriever sitting on a beige linen couch in a sunlit Canadian living room, soft natural window light from the right. Mid-shot, eye-level, shallow depth of field. The dog looks at the camera with calm, alert expression — ears up, fully visible, never cropped. Over 6 seconds the dog's image gradually transitions into a soft watercolour portrait painting style, brush textures appearing around the edges first, then blooming inward. Final frame holds the watercolour version for a beat. Cinematic, warm cream-and-honey palette, no text overlays, no logos, no people in frame, 24fps, vertical 9:16 aspect ratio.

### Prompt 2 — "Canvas on the wall reveal" (9:16, 5s)
> Camera tilts up slowly from a beige mid-century-modern couch in a softly lit Canadian living room to reveal a 16x20 gallery-wrapped canvas hanging above. The canvas shows a watercolour-style portrait of a small scruffy terrier mix, pet's full head and ears clearly visible, dog name "Biscuit" written below the pet in italic serif font. Soft late-afternoon golden light streams in from a window on the left. A real dog walks into the bottom of the frame and looks up at the canvas. No people, no text on screen, no logos. Cinematic, warm palette, shallow depth of field, vertical 9:16, 5 seconds.

### Prompt 3 — "Three styles fast cut" (1:1, 8s)
> A square format video showing three quick transitions of the same dog photo into three different portrait styles. Frame 1 (0–2s): a real candid phone photo of a black Labrador on grass. Frame 2 (2–4s): the same dog rendered as a soft watercolour painting. Frame 3 (4–6s): the same dog rendered as bold-graphic-poster art with flat color blocks. Frame 4 (6–8s): the same dog rendered as minimal black-and-white line art on cream paper. Each transition is a smooth liquid morph, not a hard cut. No text, no logos, no humans in frame. Studio-clean cream background. Square 1:1 aspect.

### Prompt 4 — "Cat owner POV" (9:16, 8s)
> First-person POV from a person sitting on a couch, holding a smartphone in two hands. The phone screen shows the photo of an orange tabby cat. The screen UI shows a clean minimalist app interface with the photo and a single button "Generate portrait" at the bottom. The user taps the button. A subtle shimmer animation moves across the phone screen, then the image on the screen smoothly transforms into a watercolour-style portrait of the same cat. The user smiles softly (only their lower face visible). Soft natural light, neutral living room background slightly out of focus. Vertical 9:16. No music. No text overlays. 8 seconds.

### Prompt 5 — "Dog reacts to portrait" (9:16, 6s)
> A medium shot of a curious beagle sitting on a hardwood floor, looking up at someone holding a framed pet portrait of the same beagle in soft watercolour style. The dog tilts its head slightly to the side, ears perked, full head visible. The portrait is held at eye level, gallery-wrapped canvas, modest size. Warm Canadian-cottage living room background, slightly blurred, soft natural daylight. The dog walks one step closer toward the canvas mid-clip. No people's faces in frame, only hands holding the canvas. No text, no logos. Vertical 9:16, 6 seconds.

### Prompt 6 — Ambient B-roll (16:9 or 9:16, 4s loops)
> Slow camera push-in onto a stack of 4 gallery-wrapped canvas pet portraits leaning against a cream wall, each showing a different pet (dog, cat, rabbit, dog) in a different illustration style (watercolour, modern shape, line art, charcoal). All canvases have the pets' full heads visible, never cropped. Top-left of the wall has a single small "Pet Printables" wordmark sticker, otherwise no branding. Soft natural light from the left, cream and tan palette. No motion in the canvases. Static frame with subtle camera drift. 4 seconds, can loop seamlessly.

**Verify each video output for:**
1. Pet head/ears not cropped (any crop = redo)
2. No text overlays you didn't ask for (generators love to hallucinate "ART" or fake logos — re-roll if so)
3. No humans' faces (avoids likeness rights issues + keeps focus on the pet)
4. Calm pet behaviour (no aggressive lunging, no eating, no animals in distress)
5. No price tags or numbers anywhere

## Anti-friction rules (re-iterate every campaign)

1. One single landing URL. Don't fragment across multiple "join" pages.
2. Bio link goes to the funnel. Not to the rules page, not to the homepage.
3. No "DM us to enter" — drives traffic to inbox, not to the site. The whole point is on-site signal.
4. Caption first sentence must work on its own — IG cuts after ~125 chars.
5. Cover image / first frame must communicate "free portrait" within 1 second of view.
6. Every share asset that includes a portrait must have the wordmark baked in (it is, by default — the Story compose handles this).
